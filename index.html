Hi!

I'm Agniva (Bengali: অগ্নিভ, /'ogni-vo/) and welcome to my personal space.

I'm a Physics Grad student at University of Minnesota. I've done my bachelors and masters from University of Calcutta and Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur in India. I am originally from Konnagar, located in the suburbs of Kolkata, India.

In University of Minnesota, I am working with Professor Liliya Williams. My current research is primarily focussed on Strong Gravitational Lensing. The main motivation of my work lies in finding free-form non-parametric inversions of galaxy clusters using strong gravitational lensing, dark matter dynamics in galaxy clusters and predictions of precision measurement of the Hubble constant using reconstructed time delay estimations. The core theoretical work is mainly related to reconstruction of galaxy cluster mass distributions using Grale, a genetic algorithm-based non-parametric method, which runs on a supercomputer. I am also a part of the lens inversion working group in the Beyond Ultra-deep Frontier Fields And Legacy Observations (BUFFALO) project, which is an astronomical survey built around the six Hubble Space Telescope (HST) Frontier Fields clusters designed to learn about early galactic assembly and clustering and prepare targets for observations with the James Webb Space Telescope.
